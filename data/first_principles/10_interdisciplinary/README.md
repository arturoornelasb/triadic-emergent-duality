# 10 — Interdisciplinary Sciences

> *"The most exciting phrase to hear in science is not 'Eureka!' but 'That's funny...'"* — Isaac Asimov

## Scope

Interdisciplinary sciences sit at the intersection of two or more traditional domains. They often reveal **emergent principles** — first principles that cannot be fully reduced to any single parent discipline. These fields are where some of the most interesting questions about reductionism, emergence, and the unity of science arise.

## Branches Covered

| Branch | Directory | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Biophysics** | `biophysics/` | Physical principles governing biological systems, molecular motors, membrane physics |
| **Computational Biology** | `computational_biology/` | Algorithmic principles of biological data, sequence analysis, network biology |
| **Cognitive Science** | `cognitive_science/` | Computation in the brain, embodied cognition, representations |
| **Complexity Science** | `complexity_science/` | Emergence, self-organization, power laws, chaos, network theory |
| **Data Science** | `data_science/` | Statistical learning theory, bias-variance tradeoff, curse of dimensionality |
| **Philosophy of Information** | `philosophy_of_information/` | Nature of information, information as a fundamental concept |

## Research Approach

1. For each interdisciplinary field, identify:
   - Which parent disciplines contribute principles
   - Whether there are **genuinely novel principles** that emerge at the intersection
   - What the **bridge laws** are that connect the parent disciplines
2. Pay special attention to **Complexity Science** — it may contain truly universal principles (emergence, self-organization) that apply across all sciences
3. Evaluate whether information is a **fundamental concept** cutting across all sciences

## Key Questions

- Are there genuinely interdisciplinary first principles, or do they all reduce to parent fields?
- Is complexity/emergence a first principle or a consequence of simpler principles?
- Is information a fundamental concept on par with matter and energy?
- What does it mean for a principle to be "emergent"?
