# 08 — Social Sciences

> *"The curious task of economics is to demonstrate to men how little they really know about what they imagine they can design."* — F.A. Hayek

## Scope

The social sciences study human behavior, societies, and institutions. Their first principles are more contested than those of the natural sciences — they often take the form of **foundational assumptions** about human nature, rationality, and social organization rather than universal laws. Identifying what counts as a "first principle" here requires careful distinction between empirical regularities, theoretical axioms, and normative assumptions.

## Branches Covered

| Branch | Directory | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Economics** | `economics/` | Scarcity, rational choice, supply/demand, comparative advantage |
| **Psychology** | `psychology/` | Behaviorism principles, cognitive architecture, perception axioms |
| **Sociology** | `sociology/` | Social facts, structuration, collective behavior |
| **Political Science** | `political_science/` | Sovereignty, legitimacy, power, collective action |
| **Linguistics** | `linguistics/` | Universal grammar, compositionality, arbitrariness of the sign |
| **Anthropology** | `anthropology/` | Cultural universals, kinship systems, symbolic thought |

## Research Approach

1. Start with **Economics** — has the most formalized axiom systems
2. **Psychology** — foundational assumptions about cognition and behavior
3. **Linguistics** — formal principles of language
4. **Sociology**, **Political Science**, **Anthropology** — progressively less formalized
5. For each: carefully separate *axioms of the theory* from *empirical claims* from *normative commitments*
6. Note where social science principles **reduce to** vs. **emerge from** biology and psychology

## Key Questions

- Do social sciences have genuine first principles or only theoretical frameworks?
- Is rational choice theory an axiom or an approximation?
- What is the status of "laws" in social science (e.g., supply and demand)?
- How do emergent social phenomena relate to individual psychology?
- Are there true cultural universals that qualify as first principles?

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Mathematics (statistics, game theory), Biology (evolutionary psychology, neuroscience)
- **Related to:** Philosophy, Formal Sciences (decision theory, game theory)
