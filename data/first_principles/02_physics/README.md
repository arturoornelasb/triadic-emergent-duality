# 02 — Physics

> *"Physics is the most fundamental and all-inclusive of the sciences."* — Richard Feynman

## Scope

Physics is the study of matter, energy, space, time, and their interactions. It seeks to describe the behavior of the universe through **fundamental laws** expressed in mathematical language. Physics occupies a unique position: it is the most fundamental empirical science, and its principles underlie chemistry, biology, earth sciences, and engineering.

## Research Approach

1. Start with **Classical Mechanics** — the historical foundation
2. Then **Thermodynamics** — introduces statistical reasoning
3. **Electromagnetism** — completes classical physics
4. **Quantum Mechanics** and **Relativity** — the two pillars of modern physics
5. Derived branches (particle, astro, condensed matter, etc.)
6. For each branch: identify the **minimal set of postulates** from which everything follows

## Key Questions

- Can all of physics be derived from a single set of principles (a "Theory of Everything")?
- What is the relationship between symmetry and conservation laws (Noether's theorem)?
- Which physics principles are truly independent vs. derivable from deeper ones?
- What role do variational principles (least action) play as unifying first principles?
