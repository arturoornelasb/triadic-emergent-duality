# 09 — Philosophy of Science

> *"Science is what you know. Philosophy is what you don't know."* — Bertrand Russell

## Scope

Philosophy of science examines the **foundations, methods, and implications** of science itself. It asks meta-level questions: What makes something a scientific law? What justifies induction? What does it mean for a theory to be "true"? This domain provides the conceptual infrastructure that all other sciences implicitly rely on — making explicit what is usually left assumed.

## Branches Covered

| Branch | Directory | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Epistemology** | `epistemology/` | Justification of knowledge, problem of induction, scientific method |
| **Metaphysics** | `metaphysics/` | Causation, natural kinds, laws of nature, realism vs. anti-realism |
| **Philosophy of Mathematics** | `philosophy_of_mathematics/` | Platonism, formalism, intuitionism, mathematical truth |
| **Philosophy of Physics** | `philosophy_of_physics/` | Interpretation of QM, nature of spacetime, determinism |
| **Philosophy of Mind** | `philosophy_of_mind/` | Consciousness, mental causation, functionalism, qualia |
| **Philosophy of Biology** | `philosophy_of_biology/` | Species concepts, levels of selection, teleology in biology |

## Research Approach

1. Start with **Epistemology** — the foundation: how do we know anything?
2. **Metaphysics** — what exists? What are laws of nature?
3. Then domain-specific philosophies (math, physics, mind, biology)
4. For each: identify the **foundational assumptions** that working scientists implicitly accept
5. Map which philosophical positions are **required** for each science's first principles to hold

## Key Questions

- What makes something a "law of nature" vs. an accidental regularity?
- Is the problem of induction solvable, and does science need it to be?
- Are mathematical objects real (Platonism) or constructed (formalism)?
- What does quantum mechanics say about the nature of reality?
- Can consciousness be explained by physical first principles?

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Familiarity with the sciences being philosophized about
- **Required by:** Provides meta-level justification for all other domains

## Phase

**Phase 8** — Best done after substantial familiarity with the sciences themselves.
