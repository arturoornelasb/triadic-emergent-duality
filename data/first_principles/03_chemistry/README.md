# 03 — Chemistry

> *"The chemist is a guest at the physicist's table."* — attributed to various

## Scope

Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, composition, structure, and the changes it undergoes during reactions. Chemistry occupies a **bridging position** between physics and biology — its principles emerge from quantum mechanics but give rise to the molecular complexity that enables life.

## Branches Covered

| Branch | Directory | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **General Chemistry** | `general_chemistry/` | Atomic theory, periodic law, stoichiometry, chemical bonding |
| **Organic Chemistry** | `organic_chemistry/` | Carbon-based compounds, functional groups, reaction mechanisms |

| **Nuclear Chemistry** | `nuclear_chemistry/` | Radioactivity, nuclear reactions, binding energy |

## Research Approach

1. Start with **General Chemistry** — atomic theory, periodic law
2. **Physical Chemistry** and **Quantum Chemistry** — the theoretical backbone
3. Then **Organic** and **Inorganic** — structural chemistry principles
4. **Biochemistry** — bridging to biology
5. For each: distinguish principles *native to chemistry* from those *inherited from physics*

## Key Questions

- Are there genuinely chemical first principles, or does everything reduce to quantum mechanics?
- What is the status of the periodic law — empirical fact or derivable principle?
- How do emergent properties (e.g., aromaticity) relate to underlying quantum principles?
- What makes carbon special? Is this a principle or a contingent fact?

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Physics (quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism), Mathematics
- **Required by:** Biology, Earth Sciences, Environmental Science
