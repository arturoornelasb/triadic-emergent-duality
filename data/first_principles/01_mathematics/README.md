# 01 — Mathematics

> *"Mathematics is the queen of the sciences and number theory is the queen of mathematics."* — Carl Friedrich Gauss

## Scope

Mathematics is the study of abstract structures, quantities, patterns, and relationships using rigorous logical reasoning. It serves as the **foundational language** for all other sciences. Unlike empirical sciences, mathematics derives its truths through **deduction from axioms**, making its first principles particularly well-defined.

## Why Mathematics First?

Every other science in this project depends on mathematical structures. Physics uses calculus and differential equations. Chemistry uses group theory and linear algebra. Biology uses statistics and dynamical systems. By establishing mathematical first principles first, we create the bedrock upon which all other principles rest.

## Research Approach

1. Start with **Logic & Set Theory** — the absolute foundation
2. Move to **Foundations** — how mathematics justifies itself
3. Then branch-specific axioms (algebra, analysis, geometry, etc.)
4. Identify the **minimal axiom sets** that generate each branch
5. Map the **dependency graph** between branches

## Key Questions

- What are the ZFC axioms and why were they chosen?
- What does Gödel's incompleteness tell us about the limits of first principles?
- Are there genuinely independent axiom systems (e.g., Euclidean vs. non-Euclidean geometry)?
- What is the relationship between the axiom of choice and the rest of mathematics?
