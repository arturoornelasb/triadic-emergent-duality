# 04 — Biology

> *"Nothing in biology makes sense except in the light of evolution."* — Theodosius Dobzhansky

## Scope

Biology is the study of living organisms, their structure, function, growth, origin, evolution, and distribution. Biology's first principles are unique because they involve **emergence** — living systems exhibit properties that are not straightforwardly predictable from physics and chemistry alone.

## Research Approach

1. Start with **Molecular Biology** — the central dogma
2. **Cell Biology** — cell theory as a first principle
3. **Genetics** — laws of inheritance
4. **Evolutionary Biology** — natural selection and its consequences
5. Build up to systems-level principles (ecology, neuroscience, physiology)
6. Carefully distinguish **laws** from **strong generalizations** (biology has fewer strict laws than physics)

## Key Questions

- Does biology have genuine *laws* or only *strong generalizations* with exceptions?
- Is the central dogma of molecular biology a first principle or a derived consequence?
- What is the relationship between information (genetic code) and thermodynamics?
- Are there biological principles that are truly irreducible to chemistry?
- What role does contingency vs. necessity play in biological "laws"?

## Dependencies
