# 06 — Computer Science

> *"Computer science is no more about computers than astronomy is about telescopes."* — Edsger Dijkstra

## Scope

Computer Science is the study of computation, information, and automation. Its first principles span abstract mathematical foundations (computability, complexity) and engineering principles (systems design, algorithms). Computer science is unique in that many of its first principles are **mathematical theorems** with precise proofs.

## Branches Covered

| Branch | Directory | Focus |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| **Theory of Computation** | `theory_of_computation/` | Turing machines, Church-Turing thesis, halting problem, complexity classes |
| **Algorithms & Data Structures** | `algorithms_and_data_structures/` | Algorithmic paradigms, complexity bounds, fundamental data structures |
| **Information Theory** | `information_theory/` | Shannon entropy, channel capacity, data compression limits |
| **Programming Language Theory** | `programming_language_theory/` | Lambda calculus, type theory, semantics |
| **Systems & Networking** | `systems_and_networking/` | End-to-end principle, layered abstraction, CAP theorem |
| **Cryptography** | `cryptography/` | One-way functions, computational hardness assumptions, information-theoretic security |
| **Artificial Intelligence** | `artificial_intelligence/` | Rationality, learning theory, representation theorems |

## Research Approach

1. Start with **Theory of Computation** — the absolute foundation (what is computable?)
2. **Information Theory** — what is information?
3. **Algorithms** — fundamental limits and paradigms
4. **Programming Language Theory** — lambda calculus and type theory
5. **Systems**, **Cryptography**, **AI** — applied principles
6. Distinguish *proven theorems* from *engineering principles* from *heuristics*

## Key Questions

- Is the Church-Turing thesis a first principle or a physical hypothesis?
- What are the fundamental limits of computation (P vs NP, halting problem)?
- Is information physical? (Connection to physics)
- What are the irreducible principles of artificial intelligence vs. borrowed statistics?

## Dependencies

- **Requires:** Mathematics (logic, discrete math, probability, algebra)
- **Related to:** Physics (quantum computing), Formal Sciences (logic, decision theory)
